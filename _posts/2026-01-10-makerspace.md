        <p>
        This is a <strong>laser-engraved LED-lit acrylic portrait</strong> that I gifted my girlfriend for her 18th birthday. The portrait depicts our last hangout in the summer before we left for college, serving as a desk decoration and night lamp for her to commemorate our shared memories.
        <br>
        <br>
        I was pretty excited about this project because I did not have much experience with electronics and wanted to learn more about that field. <strong>To create the lighting component, I cut apart my phone charger to attach the wiring to an LED strip. This also required soldering on a 50kΩ resistor to the connection because USB-C cables only function within a certain voltage reading. With everything assembled, the cable can be plugged into a wall outlet and power the LED strip. </strong>
        <br>
        <br>
        Although the process was grueling, it was very rewarding because I was able to learn a lot about electrical engineering. <strong>Not only did I get to explore how to use many tools like the power supply, multimeter, and soldering iron, I also learned how to read the data sheets of electrical components to understand how they work, helping me diagnose and debug any issues that arose.</strong>
        </p>

        <p>This is a <strong>layered, laser-cut portrait</strong> I created for my first group of friends at MIT who are three roommates dubbed the <strong>"Omega Three Squad"</strong>. I met them during Freshman Orientation and we have been together through thick and thin since then, struggling through Motorsports, Intramural Badminton, and PSETS together.
        <br>
        <br>
        Without them, I honestly don't think I would've made it through my first semester, so <strong>for their birthdays, I drew and laser-engraved their portraits onto separate pieces of wood that fit together like a puzzle</strong>. The frame, portrait, and background are separated onto different layers glued together to produce a 3D effect. With this, each of the roommates have their own respective pieces that are incomplete without the other two, serving as a reminder for their inseparable bond as roommates and friends.
        <br>
        <br>
        Though I spent a couple late nights during finals week getting this gift done, I had a lot of fun making it. It was my first introduction to MIT's makerspace environment and I got to try out all the different laser cutters I had at my disposal. It was also really cool being able to <strong>blend my creativity with a physical product and create something meaningful for others to enjoy</strong>.
        </p>

    // Helper: Create a reusable carousel controller
    function setupCarousel(side) {
    const images = document.querySelectorAll(`.carousel-image.${side}`);
    let current = 0;
    function showImage(index) {
        images.forEach((img, i) => {
        img.style.opacity = (i === index) ? 1 : 0;
        });
    }
    document.querySelector(`.${side}-next`).addEventListener('click', () => {
        current = (current + 1) % images.length;
        showImage(current);
    });
    document.querySelector(`.${side}-prev`).addEventListener('click', () => {
        current = (current - 1 + images.length) % images.length;
        showImage(current);
    });
    }

    setupCarousel('first');
    setupCarousel('second');
    setupCarousel('third');
</script>
